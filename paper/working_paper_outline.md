# Working Paper Outline

## Title

Leveraged Buyouts in Emerging Markets: Capital Structure Adaptation, Regulatory Frictions, and the Case of India

## Abstract Draft

This paper examines how leveraged buyouts in emerging markets differ from classic US and European LBOs, using India as the core case study. We argue that Indian LBOs require adapted capital structures because of regulatory frictions, promoter ownership patterns, debt-market constraints, and exit-route differences. The paper develops a comparative framework between standard developed-market LBOs and India-adapted buyouts, supported by a hand-collected transaction database, regulatory-friction scoring, and an open-source LBO model.
