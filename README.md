# Leveraged Buyouts in Emerging Markets: India Case Study

Public research repository for the working paper:

**Leveraged Buyouts in Emerging Markets: Capital Structure Adaptation, Regulatory Frictions, and the Case of India**

## Research Objective

This project studies why Indian and emerging-market LBOs differ from classic US/European LBOs. The core argument is that emerging-market buyouts require adapted capital structures because of regulatory constraints, promoter/family ownership structures, local debt market depth, INR borrowing costs, limited use of classic acquisition leverage, private credit growth, NCD/CCD/structured-credit instruments, and different exit routes through IPOs, strategic sales, and secondary buyouts.

## Core Research Questions

1. Why are classic US-style LBOs less common in India?
2. How do Indian buyout capital structures differ from developed-market LBO structures?
3. What role do NCDs, CCDs, INR term loans, private credit, and structured finance play?
4. Do regulatory frictions materially reduce feasible leverage?
5. How should an India-adapted LBO model differ from a standard Wall Street LBO model?
