# Research Design

## Working Paper Title

**Leveraged Buyouts in Emerging Markets: Capital Structure Adaptation, Regulatory Frictions, and the Case of India**

## Central Thesis

Indian LBOs are not merely smaller versions of US or European LBOs. They require adapted capital structures because acquisition leverage is constrained by legal, regulatory, market-depth, ownership, and exit-route frictions.

## Hypotheses

### H1: Emerging-market LBOs use lower acquisition leverage than US/European LBOs.
Reason: higher borrowing costs, lower debt-market depth, regulatory complexity, and promoter ownership.

### H2: Indian LBOs rely more on hybrid/structured instruments.
Expected instruments include NCDs, CCDs, structured private credit, promoter financing, and INR-denominated term loans.

### H3: Regulatory friction lowers feasible leverage and increases equity contribution.
Relevant areas include financial-assistance rules, RBI external commercial borrowing constraints, SEBI takeover rules, stamp duty, insolvency timing, and tax treatment.

### H4: Growth assumptions matter more than financial engineering in India-adapted LBOs.
Because leverage is lower, sponsor returns depend more on revenue CAGR, EBITDA margin expansion, and exit multiple realization.

## Methodology

1. Build a hand-collected transaction database.
2. Compare Indian/EM LBO capital structures with developed-market LBO benchmarks.
3. Create a regulatory-friction scoring framework.
4. Build a standard US-style LBO model and an India-adapted LBO model.
5. Run sensitivity analysis on leverage, interest rates, EBITDA growth, tax, exit multiple, and holding period.
6. Write case studies of selected Indian buyout/control transactions.

## Expected Contribution

- First-principles explanation of India-adapted LBO mechanics.
- Public GitHub replication package.
- Open-source LBO model template for Indian/emerging-market buyouts.
- Framework useful for researchers, PE analysts, private credit investors, and finance students.
